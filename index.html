Uganda's wildlife

Welcome to the UWCS

At the Uganda Wildlife Conservation Society (UWCS), we are committed to preserving and protecting Uganda's unique and diverse wildlife. Join us in creating a future where nature and people thrive together.


Uganda is home to a stunning array of wildlife, including the iconic mountain gorillas, lions, elephants, and diverse bird species. These creatures are not only vital to the ecological balance but also form a cornerstone of Uganda's cultural heritage and tourism. Conservation is crucial to ensure the survival of these species. By protecting their habitats and promoting sustainable practices, we can safeguard Uganda's natural legacy and support communities that depend on these ecosystems.

our mission ,vision and corevalues

mission

To conserve and protect Uganda's wildlife for present and future generations through sustainable practices and community involvement.

vision

A thriving ecosystem where Uganda's rich biodiversity is preserved for generations to come.

corevalues

  • Integrity in conservation
  • Commitment to sustainability
  • Respect for biodiversity and communities
  • Innovation in wildlife protection

Conservation Projects

Uganda is actively involved in conservation projects to protect its natural heritage. Here are some notable initiatives:

Project: Gorilla Conservation

Goals: Protect mountain gorillas and their habitat.

Achievements: Increased population numbers over the past decade.

Project: Wetland Restoration

Goals: Restore degraded wetlands and promote biodiversity.

Achievements: Several hectares of wetlands restored in 2023.
